Please consider our manuscript, "Uncertainty-Aware Multi-Omics Integration and Network Perturbation Analysis in TCGA Ovarian Cancer," for publication in the International Journal of Clinical Oncology.

We present a reproducible integrative analysis of public TCGA-OV data across mutation, copy number, methylation, RNA, and optional protein layers. The work combines latent-factor integration, supervised multi-block modeling, multilayer network analysis, and perturbation experiments with bootstrap uncertainty quantification.

Major findings include:
- Core matched cohort of 90 patients (RNA/CNA/methylation/mutation), with protein-matched subset of 57.
- Best all-sample discrimination from RNA modules (AUC 0.613, 95% CI 0.489-0.737).
- Stable top hubs (LF8, LF5, LF6, LF7) with high bootstrap top-k persistence.
- Uncertainty-calibrated perturbation ranking showing largest global effects around latent-factor hubs.

We believe this manuscript aligns with IJCO’s translational oncology scope by pairing clinically interpretable endpoints with robust computational evidence.

This work is original, not under consideration elsewhere, and all authors have approved submission.
