Dear Editors,

We submit our manuscript, "Uncertainty-Calibrated Multi-Omics Network Modeling in Ovarian Cancer Identifies Stable Cross-Layer Hubs and Risk Signals," for consideration in JAMA Oncology.

Using public TCGA-OV data, we developed a fully reproducible multi-omics workflow integrating mutation, copy-number, methylation, RNA, and optional protein layers. Our analysis combines latent-factor integration, supervised multi-block modeling, and multilayer network inference, with bootstrap uncertainty quantification across predictive benchmarking, hub stability, and perturbation response.

Key findings include:
- 90 patients matched across core layers and 57 in a protein-matched subset.
- Best all-sample discrimination from RNA modules (AUC 0.613, 95% CI 0.489-0.737).
- Robust hub stability centered on latent factors LF8/LF5/LF6/LF7.
- Perturbation analyses showing consistent high-impact network responses with rank stability intervals.

We believe the work aligns with JAMA Oncology’s interest in clinically meaningful translational oncology and methodologically rigorous evidence generation.

This manuscript is original, not under consideration elsewhere, and all authors approve submission.
